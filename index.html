APJ Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015

Missile Man of India

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, fondly remembered as APJ Abdul Kalam, was an eminent Indian scientist, visionary leader, and the 11th President of India. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Kalam emerged from humble beginnings to become one of the most respected figures in Indian history.

A brilliant student with a passion for aerospace engineering, Kalam graduated from the Madras Institute of Technology in 1958. He joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and later the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), where he made significant contributions to India's space and missile programs.

Kalam's crowning achievement came with the successful development of India's indigenous satellite launch vehicle and ballistic missile systems, earning him the moniker "Missile Man of India". Under his leadership, India conducted successful nuclear tests in 1998, establishing the country as a nuclear power.

Beyond his scientific achievements, Kalam was a visionary leader who believed in harnessing technology for national development and empowering India's youth. He tirelessly advocated for education, especially in science and technology, and inspired millions with his vision of a developed and inclusive India.

In 2002, Kalam was elected as the President of India, serving a term marked by his humility, integrity, and commitment to public service. He used his presidency to connect with the youth, encouraging them to dream big and work hard to achieve their goals.

Even after his tenure as President, Kalam continued to inspire through his writings, speeches, and interactions with students and youth across the country. His books, including "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds", became bestsellers and served as guiding lights for aspiring individuals.

Tragically, Abdul Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong. His death was mourned by the nation, but his legacy lives on as a beacon of hope, courage, and dedication to the service of humanity.

National Awards

  • Bharat Ratna (1997)
  • Padma Vibhushan (1990)
  • Padma Bhushan (1981)
  • Indira Gandhi Award for National Integration (1997)
  • Honorary doctorates from over 40 universities worldwide testify to his global influence and admiration.
